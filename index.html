    My Background

    I grew up in a village in the middle of nowhere, with no one to play with, and when I went to school I used to stand behind the older kids so I could see what they were playing.
    Years passed, and I got my first computer and decided I wanted to make a game to show the kids at school, though, without an internet connection I couldn't do much.

    I was 14 years old when I managed to download Unity and install it on my computer at home.
    I was completely lost and didn't know what to do or where to start, it was then that I discovered that I had to learn to code to make a game.

    Since I had no internet at home, whenever I went to the library I took my flash drive and saved the code in a Word document so I could copy it into Visual Studio at home. This is how I completed a series of tutorials that led to my first RPG.

    The project didn't age well, since he had a very large scope.

    About a year later, I finally got internet access and started to make simple things that were enjoyable to do. My motivation ramped up as I found that the joy of programming came from seeing these little things working.

    I've learned 3D modeling software so I could make props and texture them to complete some projects.
    All I wanted was to program, and when it came to putting art into the project, new ideas would rush in, which meant more to program and learn!

    Nowadays I love programming, I find joy in seeing little things working, I hope to continue doing what I love, and learn much more in a near future.



    TL;DR:

    I grew up in the middle of nowhere, I loved to watch the boys playing games at school, I discovered Unity, I wanted to make an RPG, I gave up on doing the RPG, I have started doing small projects, and here I am!
