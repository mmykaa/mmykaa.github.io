    My Background

    I grew up in a village in the middle of nowhere, with no one to play with, and when I went to school I used to stand behind the older kids so I could see what they were playing on their PCs.

    Years passed and I got my first computer and so I decided that I would make a game to show the kids at school, but without internet, I couldn't do much.

    So the small moments that I could have internet was when I went to the library, and that's where I discovered Unity.

    I was 14 years old and managed to download unity and install it on my computer at home.
    I was completely lost, I didn't know what to do or where to start, it was then that I discovered that I had to know how to program in order to make a game.

    Since i had no internet at home, whenever I went to the library I took my flash drive and saved the code in a word document so I could copy it into visual studio at home. and this is how I completed a series of tutorials that led to my first RPG


    Obviously this didn't go well, since my project had a very large scope.


    then about a year later I finally got internet access and... I gave up on the idea of the best rpg people would ever see! and started making little things, and found that the joy of programming comes from seeing the little things work.

    With this I always tried to imagine complete games to make, so I learned 3D software so I could model props and texture them to complete the game. But on my own I was never able to do it, because I wanted to program, and when it came to putting art into the game I would get new ideas and that meant there was more to do and more to program!

    Nowadays I love programming, I find joy in seeing little things work, i hope to continue doing what i like, and learn much more than what i currently know.





    TL;DR: I grew up in the middle of nowhere, I loved to watch the boys playing games at school, I discovered Unity, I wanted to make an RPG, I gave up on doing the RPG, I started doing small projects, and here i am!
